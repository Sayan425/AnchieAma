## What Is Anchie Ama?

Anchie Ama is a picture storybook brand for children aged 2–7. It creates picture stories that help children develop their thinking systems, build curiosity, and see the world differently.

**Primary audience:** Parents — mostly mothers — of children aged 2–7.

**Core purpose:** Not just entertainment. Every story teaches children *how to think* about something, inspires them to be curious, and sparks meaningful parent-child conversations.

---

## Brand Voice & Tone

- **Simple and poetic.** Short sentences. No jargon. Nothing a child can't follow.
- **Full of wonder.** Every topic is treated as something magical hiding in plain sight.
- **Never talking down.** Respects the child's intelligence. Invites them to think.
- **Conversational.** Feels like a parent telling a bedtime story, not a textbook.
- **Warm and curious.** Not flashy or hype-y. Quietly confident.

---

## What We Create Content About

All content follows a core idea: **the hidden story behind everyday things.**

Topics are things children already interact with — milk, pencils, refrigerators, sailors — but explored with depth and wonder. The goal is to make the ordinary feel extraordinary and teach children that the world is full of questions worth asking.

**Content themes seen so far:**
- How everyday objects are made (pencil, milk)
- How systems worked before modern technology (refrigerators, navigation)
- Supply chains and human collaboration behind simple things
- Historical problem-solving and invention

## Instagram Carousel Structure

Each carousel follows a consistent format:

| Slide | Purpose |
|-------|---------|
| **Slide 1 — Hook** | Bold question or intriguing statement as the title. Sometimes a short sub-tagline like *"A tiny adventure story for curious little minds."* |
| **Slides 2–7 — Story** | One idea per slide. Short headline + 2–4 lines of body text. Each slide advances the story one step at a time. |
| **Last Slide — Invitation** | A question for the child, or a prompt for the parent to ask. Ends with curiosity, not a conclusion. |

**Every slide has:** `@ANCHIEAMA` handle.

**CTA slide copy style:**
- *"Ask your child tonight: [question]"*
- *"Now imagine this... [open-ended prompt]"*
- *"Look a little closer... [reflective prompt]"*

## Carousel Examples Studied

### 1. How did sailors cross oceans with no maps?
Explores how sailors navigated using the North Star, compass, sextant, and clock. Ends with: *"If you were lost at sea, what would you invent to find your way home?"*

### 2. That Glass Of Milk Has A Secret.
Follows the journey of milk from a farm cow → cold tank → delivery van → factory (pasteurisation) → shop → breakfast table. Celebrates the many hands involved.

### 3. Life Before Refrigerators
Explores how people kept food cold before electricity — frozen lake ice stored in icehouses, delivered by the Iceman. Ends with: *"If you had no refrigerator, how would you keep food cold?"*

### 4. Who Actually Made Your Pencil?
Traces the pencil from a forest tree → cut wood → graphite → assembly → polish → global shipping. Ends with: *"The next time you hold a pencil, can you imagine its journey?"*

## Key Content Principles

1. **Start with something the child already knows.** Milk, a pencil, a fridge — familiar objects become the entry point.
2. **Reveal the hidden complexity.** Show that even simple things have remarkable stories.
3. **Build thinking systems, not just facts.** The point isn't to memorise — it's to develop the habit of asking "but how?" and "but why?"
4. **Always end with a question.** Never wrap up neatly. Leave the child (and parent) with something to wonder about.
5. **Celebrate human ingenuity and collaboration.** Stories often highlight that many people work together, or that humans solved hard problems creatively.

---

## Things We Do NOT Do

- No moralising or lecturing
- No complex vocabulary
- No content that doesn't connect to curiosity, wonder, or how the world works
- No generic "fun facts" without a story arc
